💭 My QA Testing Perspective

How a Developer Background Shapes My Approach to QA:

  • Builder's Instinct: Having built mobile apps myself, I naturally think about edge cases and where logic tends to break — even without access to the source code. It shapes how I read requirements and write test scenarios.
  • Precise Bug Reporting: My development background helps me describe not just what went wrong, but under what conditions it fails — making handoffs between QA and dev clearer and faster.
  • Adaptable Testing Approach: From manual testing on staging apps to automating repetitive flows with Selenium, Katalon, or Playwright — I work with the tools and access available, and stay thorough regardless of constraints.
  • Cross-Team Communication: Familiar with both development and business logic perspectives, I can translate technical findings into language that both developers and system analysts understand.
  • Financial & Business Risk Awareness: Testing payment and billing systems at enterprise scale taught me to prioritize accuracy in high-stakes scenarios — where a miscalculation directly impacts end customers.

"My approach to quality is a habit — built from years of understanding both how systems are made and where they break."
